1. Abstract

This report presents findings from a cross-disciplinary analysis connecting archaeoacoustics, neuroscience, quantum consciousness theory, and UAP (Unidentified Aerial Phenomenon) research. The analysis identifies a consistent 110 Hz acoustic signature across multiple ancient sites worldwide, correlating this frequency with documented neurological effects that match UAP encounter phenomenology. The findings suggest ancient civilizations may have developed architectural instruments to interface with a natural phenomenon that modern physics is only beginning to understand.

Key Finding: A 110 Hz acoustic frequency, engineered into ancient sites spanning 6,000 years and 4 continents, produces neurological effects that match the phenomenological profile of UAP encounters. This correlation, combined with Penrose-Hameroff quantum consciousness theory, suggests a potential mechanism for consciousness-phenomenon interaction.

2. Introduction

This investigation began with a simple question: What can modern analysis reveal about ancient architectural choices that previous research has overlooked?

The investigation drew from three primary domains:
